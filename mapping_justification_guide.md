# Accreditation Outcome Mapping Guide: Justification of Correlation Levels

This document provides a comprehensive guide to understanding how the automated system assigns correlation levels between Course Outcomes (COs) and Program Outcomes (POs) or Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs). 

The system maps outcomes on a standard scale of 1 to 3, adhering to established accreditation best practices. The mapping evaluated on two core dimensions: **Keyword/Cognitive Alignment** (using Bloom’s Taxonomy) and **Contextual/Semantic Integration**.

---

## 1. Definition of Mapping Levels

The correlation levels represent the strength and depth of alignment between what a course teaches (CO) and what the degree program expects the student to achieve (PO/PSO).

*   **Level 3 (Substantial / High):** The Course Outcome directly and strongly addresses the core intent of the Program Outcome. The cognitive complexity and context of the CO are a perfect match for the PO.
*   **Level 2 (Moderate / Medium):** The Course Outcome addresses a significant portion of the Program Outcome, but lacks the full depth, completeness, or exact cognitive focus required for a maximum score.
*   **Level 1 (Slight / Low):** The Course Outcome touches upon the Program Outcome tangentially or implicitly. It acts as a supporting foundation rather than directly addressing the primary goal of the PO.
*   **Level 0 / Blank (No Correlation):** The Course Outcome has no logical or significant connection to the Program Outcome.

---

## 2. Evaluation Methodology

When evaluating a CO against a PO/PSO, the system generates its justification based on the following two criteria:

### A. Basis of Keyword & Cognitive Alignment (Lexical)
This criteria examines the specific action verbs (derived from Bloom's Taxonomy) and domain-specific terminology used in both statements.
*   **For Level 3:** Direct alignment of high-level cognitive verbs. If the PO requires "Design" or "Evaluate," the CO must contain verbs of equal cognitive weight (e.g., "Create," "Propose," "Assess").
*   **For Level 2:** Related but cognitively milder verbs. If the PO requires "Design," a CO that asks students to "Apply" or "Analyze" demonstrates a moderate, but not complete, cognitive match.
*   **For Level 1:** Weak or foundational keyword overlap. If the PO requires "Design," a CO that asks students to "Identify" or "Define" shows only a slight, foundational connection.
*   **For Level 0:** No meaningful keyword overlap. The verbs and domain terms in the CO have little to no relation to the requirements set by the PO.

### B. Basis of Contextual Alignment (Semantic)
This criteria ignores the isolated words and looks at the overall meaning, scope, and application scenario of the entire sentence.
*   **For Level 3:** The practical application and scenario described in the CO fully encompass the demands of the PO. The core objective of the CO *is* the core objective of the PO.
*   **For Level 2:** The context is relevant, but the scope of the CO is narrower. For instance, the CO might solve a simple problem, whereas the PO expects the solution of "complex engineering problems."
*   **For Level 1:** The context shares a loose theme. The CO might provide background theoretical knowledge that is indirectly useful for the PO, but it is not applied in the intended context.
*   **For Level 0:** The context is entirely unrelated. The application scenario of the CO shares no theme, foundational benefit, or logical connection with the PO.

## 3. Practical Examples of Justifications

Below are examples of how the system justifies its mapping decisions to help faculty interpret the results:

### 🟢 Example of a Level 3 (Substantial) Mapping
**Course Outcome:** "Design and implement a relational database schema for a complex enterprise application."
**Program Outcome (PO3 - Design/Development of Solutions):** "Design solutions for complex engineering problems..."

*   **Mapping Level:** Level 3
*   **Justification:** 
    *   **Keyword Basis:** Direct alignment of high-level Bloom's taxonomy verbs ("Design", "Implement") with the PO's core requirements ("Design", "Solutions"). Both explicitly contain the terminology "complex."
    *   **Context Basis:** The cognitive requirement of the CO mandates students to actively create a functional solution (database schema) for a complex scenario, which directly and fundamentally satisfies the exact intent and scope of PO3.

### 🟡 Example of a Level 2 (Moderate) Mapping
**Course Outcome:** "Apply SQL queries to retrieve and filter data from multiple tables."
**Program Outcome (PO3 - Design/Development of Solutions):** "Design solutions for complex engineering problems..."

*   **Mapping Level:** Level 2
*   **Justification:**
    *   **Keyword Basis:** The CO action verb ("Apply") represents a lower cognitive level on Bloom's Taxonomy compared to the PO's requirement ("Design"). 
    *   **Context Basis:** While writing SQL queries is a vital applied skill in developing software solutions (PO3 context), it is a supportive implementation skill rather than the comprehensive "design of a complex solution" from scratch. 

### 🔴 Example of a Level 1 (Slight) Mapping
**Course Outcome:** "Describe the fundamental architecture components of a database management system."
**Program Outcome (PO5 - Modern Tool Usage):** "Create, select, and apply appropriate techniques, resources, and modern engineering and IT tools..."

*   **Mapping Level:** Level 1
*   **Justification:**
    *   **Keyword Basis:** Minimal keyword overlap. The CO emphasizes "Describe" (Foundational/Understanding), while the PO focuses on "Apply" and "Modern Tools."
    *   **Context Basis:** Understanding the architecture of a tool (DBMS) provides theoretical background knowledge that might eventually assist a student in using modern tools. However, the CO only requires description rather than the practical application demanded by the PO, resulting in a generalized, low correlation.

### ⚪ Example of a Level 0 (No Correlation) Mapping
**Course Outcome:** "Communicate effectively in written and oral forms during team presentations."
**Program Outcome (PO3 - Design/Development of Solutions):** "Design solutions for complex engineering problems..."

*   **Mapping Level:** Level 0 (Blank)
*   **Justification:**
    *   **Keyword Basis:** No relevant keyword correlation. The CO focuses on communication verbs ("Communicate", "Present"), while the PO focuses on engineering verbs ("Design", "Develop").
    *   **Context Basis:** The CO entirely addresses soft skills, teamwork, and communication (which would map highly to PO9/Teamwork or PO10/Communication), but lacks any context regarding technical problem-solving or system design needed for PO3. Therefore, no mapping is established.
